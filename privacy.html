Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

TwentyOne ("the app", "we", "us") is a mobile application for building 21-day habits. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

1. Information We Collect

When you sign in with Google, we receive the following from your Google account:

  • Your name and email address
  • Your Google profile photo (if available)

During normal app use, we store in your personal Firestore database:

  • Daily check-in data (mood, routine completion)
  • Notification preferences (chosen reminder time)
  • Avatar and display name customisation

2. How We Use Your Information

All collected data is used exclusively to provide and personalise the app experience:

  • Identifying your account and syncing your data across devices
  • Displaying your habit progress and history
  • Sending local notifications at your chosen time

We do not use your data for advertising or share it with third parties.

3. Data Storage

Your data is stored in Google Firebase (Firestore), operated by Google LLC. Data is tied to your account and is only accessible by you. Firebase infrastructure is GDPR-compliant.

4. Google Sign-In

TwentyOne uses Google Sign-In via Firebase Authentication. By signing in with Google, you agree to Google's Privacy Policy. We only request the minimum OAuth scopes needed: your email and basic profile.

5. Local Notifications

The app schedules daily reminders locally on your device. No notification content is transmitted to our servers or any third party.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

Your data is retained as long as your account is active. To delete your account and all associated data, contact us at the email below. We will process deletion within 30 days.

7. Children's Privacy

TwentyOne is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

8. Your Rights (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area, you have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal data. You may also withdraw consent at any time by deleting your account.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy occasionally. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

Contact

For any privacy-related questions or data deletion requests, reach us via GitHub Issues.
